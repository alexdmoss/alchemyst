Electron Transfer Reactions Electron transfer reactions may occur by two mechanisms: outer or inner sphere. In principle outer sphere mechanism involves electron transfer from reductant to oxidant with the coordination shells or spheres of each staying intact. That is one reactant becomes involved in the outer or second coordination sphere of the other reactant and an electron flows from the reductant to oxidant. Such a mechanism is established when rapid electron transfer occurs between two substitution-inert complexes. An inner sphere mechanism is one in which the reactant and oxidant share a ligand in their inner or primary coordination spheres the electron being transferred across a bridging group.
